The greenhouse effect is a natural process that helps keep the Earth warm enough
to support life. When sunlight reaches the Earth, some of it is absorbed by the 
surface and the rest is reflected back toward space as heat. Greenhouse gases such 
as carbon dioxide, methane, and water vapor trap part of this heat in the atmosphere, 
preventing it from escaping completely. This trapped heat maintains the Earth’s average 
temperature and makes the planet habitable. However, human activities like burning fossil 
fuels and deforestation have increased the concentration of these gases, intensifying the 
greenhouse effect. As a result, more heat is trapped, leading to global warming and climate
change, which can cause rising sea levels, extreme weather conditions, and disturbances to ecosystems.
